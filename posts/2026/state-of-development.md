### Anxiety 

For the past few months, anxiety has been always my dominant feeling. Always in the background. Every few days another voice joins this movement and share: everything has changed. Tools are obsolete, the way how organizations work is outdated, team dynamics, markets, workforce. As opposed to previous dramatic changes in our profession, it's no longer clear if it's possible to adapt for most of us.

### AI Assistance all day long

Business as usual, it's no longer experimental. It's essential part of most of workflows now.

### Frontier models are still the same

It's time of Anthropic Claude 3.6 and GPT-5.4 are gold standard and game changers. Gemini 3.1 family is good, but rarely used in my bubble for agentic coding. Used though in  many other places.  

### Super fast inference

A glimpse of faster inference is here. It's easy to be impressed by it. But you don't want to give up your best possible model. _What if it miss something and introduce more bugs?_ For small safe tasks it's easier to edit a color of a button with them, than opening the file.

### Black magic and art of satisfying agent needs

Claude Code shipping tens of features each month. You can get certification for mastering it. Some people created cheat sheet that takes full A4 of commands. People share their lifehacks. If you have a bad experience it's a skill issue, and jokes being made that it's literal now. 
Have you ever seen all ceremony and instructions and custom CLIs in a big project? 

### Cursor
 
Cursor is not that fancy thing anymore, despite still being very impressive technically and product-wise. Hype was so strong a year ago. As IDE, there are better ones, and then again for agents I still use advanced terminal setup (occasionally trying new things, like Air). 

### In pursiut of autonomous agents

We see Anthropic, OpenAI and Cursor experimenting with it. Days and weeks efforts where an agent does a very big project with as little of human intevention as possible. Competition of can be replicated, so data and distribution is important as ever. As we develop more sophisticated frameworks for such agents, probably a completely new browser engine or operating system will be possible.

### Open Source is changing

Sending PR now is not limited by your familiarity of the project. Projects like appearing. 

### Hangover after fake productivity

This is what I experienced after initial boost to "finish some apps I always wanted to finish". I got very little satification looking at the final result. It's some kind of depreceation of side projects. 

### Random notes

- Developing native macOS apps is incredibly frustrating. Can't get anything done that is _nice to use_ without very detailed spec. 
- Without oversight things can go in a wrong direction quite fast. You should either code review everything thoroughly or invent a sophisticated verification pipelines that takes as much time and constantly changing
- As everyone says: someone should decide what to build and what not to build, to keep a balance. 
- You have bigger pace when doing something on your own. But only if you know youself what to build. Syncronization is pain. But only on code level. If you want to build the real product, the product and QA is even more important. 

### We still have hope. 

We still have work.
