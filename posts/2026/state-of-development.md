March 2026. 

### Frontier models: it's still Anthropic and Open AI

It's time of Anthropic Claude 3.6 and GPT-5.4. Gemini 3.1 Pro and Flash are good, but rarely used in my bubble for agentic coding. Used for many other places.  

### Super fast inference

A glimpse of faster inference here and there. It's easy to be impressed by them. But you don't want to give up your best possible model. _What if it miss something and introduce more bugs?_ For small safe tasks it's easier to edit a color of a button with them, than opening the file.

### Black magic of satisfying agents 

Claude Code shipping tens of features each month. You can get certification for mastering it. Some people created cheat sheet that takes full A4 of commands.

### Cursor
 
Cursor is not that fancy anymore, despite still being very impressive technically and product-wise. I just find it very little reason to launch. As IDE, IntelliJ is still better, and then I just use advanced terminal setup (occasionally trying new things, like Air).

Random notes:

- Developing native macOS apps is incredibly frustrating. Can't get anything done that is _nice to use_ without very detailed spec. 
- Without oversight things can go in a wrong direction quite fast. You should either code review everything thoroughly or invent a sophisticated verification pipelines that takes as much time and constantly changing
- As everyone says: someone should decide what to build and what not to build, to keep a balance. 
- Doing something on your own gives you more pace, syncronization is a pain. But only on code level. If you want to build the real product, the product and QA is even more important.
